Welcome to Mood Tracker

Your guide to navigating and using the application effectively.

Getting Started

To begin using the Mood Tracker, simply invoke it with "Hey Alexa, open mood tracker".

Logging Your Mood

You can log your current mood by saying "I am feeling happy" or "I feel stressed". The application supports various moods such as happy, sad, stressed, and excited.

Mood Intensity

Specify the intensity of your mood on a scale of 1 to 10 by saying "It's a 5" or "About a 7".

Mood Details

Add more context to your mood log by providing details. For example, "The reason is I got a new job" or "It's because I'm moving to a new city".

Reviewing Your Mood Log

Request a summary of your mood over a specific timeframe or details for a particular day. Use commands like "give me the summary for the last 2 weeks" or "what was my mood on Tuesday in week 3".

User Interaction Scenarios

Scenario 1: First-Time User Experience

Utterance: "Alexa, open Mood Tracker."

Situation: The user is interacting with the skill for the first time.

Response: "Welcome to Mood Tracker! I can help you track your emotional well-being over time."

Prompt: "Before we start, would you like a quick overview of how I work, or would you prefer to dive right in and log your current mood?"

If the user asks for an overview:

Response: "With Mood Tracker, you can log your mood, view past entries, and gain insights into your mood patterns. To log your mood, simply tell me how you're feeling."

Scenario 2: Daily Mood Logging

Utterance: "Alexa, I want to record my mood."

Situation: The user wants to log their mood as part of their daily routine.

Response: "I'm here to help with that. Tell me, how are you feeling today?"

Prompt: "You can say 'I feel' followed by your mood."

After the user provides their mood:

Response: "You said you're feeling [user's mood], is that right?"

Prompt: "On a scale from 1 to 10, how intense is this feeling?"

Following the rating:

Response: "Got it. I've logged that you're feeling [user's mood] with an intensity of [user's rating]."

Prompt: "Would you like to add any details or is that everything for now?"

Scenario 3: Reviewing Past Entries

Utterance: "Alexa, what's my mood history for the last week?"

Situation: The user wants to review their mood logs from the past week.

Response: "Let's take a look at your mood history from this past week."

Prompt: "You had [number of happy days] days where you felt happy and [number of sad days] days with some blues. Would you like me to go into more detail?"

If the user says yes:

Response: "On Monday, you were feeling great, rating an 8 out of 10. Tuesday was a bit rougher, with a mood rating of 3. [Continue with the rest of the days]..."

Prompt: "That's the recap of your week. Would you like to analyze any day in particular?"
